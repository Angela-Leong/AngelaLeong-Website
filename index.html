Psychology

I am a psychology major at the University of California, Riverside. However, I am also currently exploring a double major in biology.

My most recent past occupation was serving as an assistant coach for a girls’ club volleyball team. It was my duty to guide the team of twelve- and thirteen-year old girls to develop beginning volleyball skills. This was done through running drills and leading practices when the head coach was absent.

Prior to that, I served as an office/marketing manager for a local artist in my hometown named Judy Gittelsohn. It was my responsibility to handle operations of the art studio such as inventory control, order management, filing, and pricing of artwork. I also published monthly newsletters that Ms. Gittelsohn sent out to various subscribers she had met in the industry. Finally, I managed content and maintained the artist’s promotional and e-commerce websites.

My future goals include attending graduate school to become a certified Physician Assistant. This career piqued my interest because I knew I wanted to work in the health industry. Through further research, I discovered that becoming a Physician Assistant would better suit my plans for my life because of the ability to have a flexible schedule. The time commitment is far less than becoming a doctor, especially when taking into account the additional schooling and residency that aspiring doctors must undergo. In addition, Physician Assistants can still carry out many similar tasks to those of doctors. My hope is that my undergraduate education will prepare me to become a PA.
